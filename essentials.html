ESSENTIAL TOOLS TO
GET STARTED

Every wannabe home mechanic should get started from somewhere and this guide will help you do it.

1

Multibit screwdriver

Ah what a beauty. The multibit screwdriver is one of the most useful and cost effective tools you can get yourself. You can get so much done with just that. Everything from laptops, phones and basically most of the things you need to get into can be conquered with that incredible tool. It is also modular which can be incredibly cost saving if you always forget your tools like I do, because when you forget a bit you can just buy a new one cheap.

That doesn’t mean it’s all good though. Firstly, it’s expensive at first. Usually much more than a regular screwdriver. Also even though as we mentioned above bits are cheap, they are SO EASY TO LOSE. You can’t get away without losing one if you are regularly using it. It’s just a part of the experience :|. Although we have a tip for minimizing that too ;).

2

Adjustable wrench

The wrench basically does the things that the screwdriver does not. It’s the second most common tool you will need for your home fixes. Everything more sturdy usually requires some size of a wrench. And with an adjustable wrench you can have a big variety of wrench sizes in one which is way cheaper than buying them all.

However it really isn’t good if you are using it regularly. It gets off the nuts, rounds them off so often and you can really go crazy if you are using it for big torque* nuts. So if you are a passionate mechanic I recommend getting a proper set of wrenches.

*hard to unscrew or requiring a lot of force to screw in
3

Toolbox/Organizer

Basically if you don’t want to lose everything you bought you will need a box. Keeping your collection of drivers, screws, and bolts in an easy-to-haul toolbox keeps things organized and handy. Also a separated tray for keeping your screws organized, because not being able to put the thing you fixed together is kind of a problem.
